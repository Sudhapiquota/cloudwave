Key Differences Between Salesforce Lightning and Classic Experience

Salesforce is a leading customer relationship management (CRM) platform that helps businesses manage their sales, marketing, and customer service operations. Over the years, Salesforce has evolved significantly, with one of the most notable changes being the introduction of Salesforce Lightning Experience. While Salesforce Classic remains available, many businesses are making the transition to Lightning to take advantage of its enhanced features and capabilities. In this blog, we'll explore the key differences between Salesforce Lightning and Classic Experience to help you understand which version is best suited for your business needs.

1. User Interface and Design

Salesforce Classic:

  • The Classic interface has a traditional, text-heavy layout that can appear cluttered.
  • It uses a tab-based navigation system, which can be less intuitive for new users.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Lightning Experience features a modern, visually appealing interface with a focus on usability.
  • It uses a more dynamic, app-based navigation system, making it easier for users to find and use features.
  • Lightning components allow for a more customizable and interactive user experience.

2. Productivity Features

Salesforce Classic:

  • Limited to standard Salesforce features without much room for customizations aimed at enhancing productivity.
  • Users rely heavily on Visualforce pages for custom functionality.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Includes productivity-boosting features such as Kanban boards for opportunity management, Path for sales processes, and enhanced calendar functionalities.
  • Lightning App Builder allows users to create custom pages and layouts without needing extensive coding knowledge.
  • Dynamic pages and components adapt to different user roles and profiles, providing a personalized experience.

3. Mobile Experience

Salesforce Classic:

  • The Classic interface is not optimized for mobile devices.
  • Users typically rely on the Salesforce1 mobile app for mobile access, which has limitations compared to the desktop version.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Lightning Experience is designed with a mobile-first approach, ensuring a seamless transition between desktop and mobile devices.
  • The Salesforce mobile app offers a consistent experience with the desktop version, including access to custom Lightning components.

4. Reports and Dashboards

Salesforce Classic:

  • Reports and dashboards in Classic are functional but lack the advanced features available in Lightning.
  • Users may find it more challenging to create visually appealing and interactive dashboards.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Lightning provides enhanced reporting and dashboard capabilities, including more chart types, interactive filters, and the ability to create dynamic dashboards.
  • Users can easily customize reports and dashboards to display real-time data and insights.

5. AppExchange and Third-Party Integrations

Salesforce Classic:

  • Classic supports AppExchange apps and third-party integrations, but some newer apps are designed exclusively for Lightning.
  • Limited capabilities for integrating advanced functionalities without custom development.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Lightning Experience is fully compatible with the latest AppExchange apps and third-party integrations.
  • Many new apps and features are developed with Lightning in mind, offering better performance and integration capabilities.

6. Customization and Development

Salesforce Classic:

  • Customization in Classic often requires extensive use of Visualforce and Apex code.
  • Limited to traditional development methods, which can be time-consuming and complex.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Lightning offers a range of low-code and no-code customization options, including the Lightning App Builder and Lightning Flow.
  • Developers can still use Apex and Visualforce, but Lightning Web Components (LWC) provide a modern framework for building custom functionality.
  • Enhanced development tools streamline the process of creating and deploying custom apps.

7. Future Updates and Support

Salesforce Classic:

  • Salesforce continues to support Classic but focuses new feature development on Lightning Experience.
  • Businesses using Classic may miss out on the latest innovations and improvements.

Salesforce Lightning:

  • Salesforce prioritizes updates and new features for Lightning Experience.
  • Businesses using Lightning benefit from regular enhancements, improved security, and the latest technological advancements.

Transitioning to Salesforce Lightning

Salesforce Lightning Experience offers a modern, intuitive interface with advanced features that enhance productivity, customization, and mobile access. While Salesforce Classic remains a viable option, especially for businesses with extensive customizations or a preference for the traditional layout, the future of Salesforce lies with Lightning. Transitioning to Lightning Experience can provide your business with the tools and capabilities needed to stay competitive and maximize the value of your Salesforce investment.
